O2DTD: Off-road Open Desert Trail Detection

A novel dataset for autonomous navigation in arid unstructured environments

O2DTD Dataset Overview

The overarching goal of the O2DTD dataset is to help advance autonomous driving and navigation systems for traversing less focused arid landscapes.

Motivation

Free space detection plays a vital role in autonomous vehicle technology for both perception and navigation. Over the past decade, it has been established that deep learning-based approaches to free space identification may be reliably applied in the real world. However, these efforts primarily focused on on-road urban settings, and few algorithms were developed for free space detection in off-road contexts. Nonetheless, the available off-road datasets and research do not significantly emphasize desert traversability analysis. Consequently, it is necessary to explore how well self-driving cars perform in harsh unstructured, and arid environments. To this end, we present a new dataset named Off-Road Open Desert Trail Detection (O2DTD), the first dataset focused on desert free space detection. The dataset was collected with six different light conditions (dawn, morning, afternoon, sunset, twilight, and night).

O2DTD Dataset Features

First Dataset for Desert Free Space Detection

The O2DTD dataset is the first dataset curated to detect desert trails, promoting the off-road autonomous navigation and driving field.

Collected in Natural Desert Environment

The O2DTD dataset is collected in Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates, in an open desert space at 24°22.5'N latitude and 54°34.5'E longitude.

Cover Six Different Light Conditions

The O2DTD dataset covers six different light conditions (dawn, morning, afternoon, sunset, twilight, and night) affecting autonomous navigation.

Easy-to-Use Data and Labels Format

All the images and annotations in the O2DTD dataset are stored in PNG format, enabling the researchers to use them for their research easily.

Over 5,000 Images with Pixel Level Annotations

The O2DTD dataset contains over 5,000 images with pixel-level annotations of three classes (sky, ground, and trail) to train and evaluate the algorithms.

Off-road Desert Trail Segmentation Benchmark

We also present the segmentation benchmark called DTSNet, which is trained using various loss functions, optimizers, and learning rates.

O2DTD Dataset Description

We gathered a dataset that includes 5,045 RGB images captured under six distinct lighting conditions: dawn, morning, afternoon, sunset, twilight, and night. These images are categorized into three classes: sky (blue), ground (yellow), and trail (gray) regions. The details of the dataset, including the image count and classes, can be found in the table below.

The dataset contains images and corresponding ground truth labels, all saved in PNG format with a resolution of 576 × 768 for convenience. To facilitate model training, validation, and testing, we have randomly divided the dataset based on illumination conditions, allocating 70% for training, 10% for validation, and 20% for testing. Both the images and labels are stored in separate sub-folders within the main directory. Additionally, the ground truth labels are available in two formats: single channel and RGB.
